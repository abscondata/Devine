# RSYN 720 Capstone Evaluation Policy

This policy defines the evaluation architecture for RSYN 720 (Senior Thesis / Integrated Synthesis). It establishes auditable standards for assessment, stage advancement, and final completion. It does not alter course structure or requirements.

## Purpose
RSYN 720 is the culminating capstone. This policy ensures that evaluation is severe, transparent, and auditable, and that the capstone remains distinct from RSYN 710’s method formation.

## Source Taxonomy (Hierarchical)
Sources are evaluated by a hierarchical model.

1. Primary Sources (authoritative evidence)
2. Magisterial Sources (subset of primary)
3. Non-magisterial primary sources (subset of primary)
4. Secondary Sources (interpretive support)

Rules:
1. Primary sources are necessary and determinative.
2. Magisterial sources carry authoritative weight and are evaluated distinctly within the primary class.
3. Secondary sources may clarify but cannot substitute for primary sources.

## Stage-Level Advancement Thresholds
Each deliverable is a blocking stage. The student advances only after meeting the threshold and avoiding fail overrides.

Default thresholds per deliverable:
1. Pass to advance: 80 or above.
2. Provisional pass: 70–79 with required revision memo.
3. Mandatory resubmission: below 70.

Fail overrides apply regardless of numeric score and block advancement.

## Fail Overrides (Global)
Any of the following triggers mandatory resubmission:
1. Missing required source category or failure of source governance.
2. Absence of required structural elements for the deliverable.
3. Evidence of misrepresentation or plagiarism.
4. Required lane integration missing or merely decorative.

## Whole-Course Weighting
1. Thesis question and scope statement: 10%
2. Annotated bibliography and method memo: 15%
3. Thesis prospectus: 15%
4. Draft thesis with objections and replies: 15%
5. Final thesis: 35%
6. Synthesis reflection: 10%

## Synthesis Reflection Gatekeeping Rule
The synthesis reflection is a hard gate. Failure triggers mandatory resubmission regardless of overall course score.

Required objections in the defense:
1. Philosophical objection
2. Scriptural objection
3. Doctrinal (magisterial or conciliar) objection
4. Historical objection

Replies must be grounded in primary sources.

## Final Course Completion Standard
To complete RSYN 720, all of the following must be true:
1. All stages passed (or provisional pass with revisions satisfied).
2. No fail overrides remain active.
3. The final thesis integrates all four lanes: philosophy, Scripture, doctrine, history.
4. The synthesis reflection passes as a gatekeeping deliverable.
